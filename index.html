Dr. Muhammad Yunus is a Bangladeshi social entrepreneur, banker, and economist, best known as the pioneer of microcredit and microfinance. Born on June 28, 1940, in Chittagong, Bangladesh, he pursued his undergraduate studies at Dhaka University and later earned a PhD in economics from Vanderbilt University, USA, through a Fulbright Scholarship. He also worked as an assistant professor of economics at Tennessee State University before returning to Bangladesh to serve as the head of the Economics Department at Chittagong University.In 1976, Dr. Yunus founded Grameen Bank, introducing the concept of providing collateral-free microloans to impoverished individuals, particularly women, to promote financial independence and reduce poverty. This groundbreaking initiative became a global model for poverty alleviation. His efforts were recognized worldwide, and in 2006, Dr. Yunus and Grameen Bank jointly received the Nobel Peace Prize for their contributions to social and economic development. Over the years, Dr. Yunus has received numerous other prestigious honors, including the Presidential Medal of Freedom and the Congressional Gold Medal from the United States. In August 2024, amidst political unrest in Bangladesh, he took on the role of Chief Adviser of Bangladesh, stepping into political leadership after decades of work in socioeconomic reform.
